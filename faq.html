01General

About the home.

What about visiting?
We operate an Open Door Policy. There are no fixed visiting hours — family and friends are welcome from early morning through to the evening (typically 8am–8pm). For visits outside those hours, particularly for end-of-life care, please call ahead.
Are you regulated, and what's your CQC rating?
Yes. Broad Oak Manor is regulated by the Care Quality Commission. Our overall rating is Good — with the inspectors awarding us Outstanding in the Caring domain. You can read the full breakdown on our About page, or read the full CQC report on the CQC website.
How long has Broad Oak Manor been a care home?
Since 1984. Broad Oak Manor began when our family couldn't find the kind of care we wanted for the people we loved — so we built it ourselves. Forty-plus years on, it's still family-owned, still in the same Queen Anne house, and still run on the same principles.
02Nursing Home

Care in the manor.

How much does it cost?
Fees depend on the size of the room, the level of nursing care required, and whether the stay is permanent or respite. We are straightforward about pricing and will give you a written quote tailored to your circumstances shortly after a viewing. There are no hidden charges — what we quote is what you pay.
How quickly can someone move in?
If a room is available and the assessment is straightforward, a placement can be arranged within a few days — including discharges from hospital. Our care manager will assess in person (or remotely from a ward), discuss with the family, and write a care plan before the move-in date.
Do you take residents with dementia?
Yes. Our nursing team has specialist dementia experience, and the manor is laid out in a way that supports residents living with cognitive change — small communal spaces, familiar staff, and a calm rhythm to the day.
What if needs change over time?
Our nursing care is designed to grow with you. As needs become more complex — including end-of-life — you stay in the room you know, with the team you know. We don't move people on. Continuity of place and people is, in our experience, one of the greatest comforts we can offer.
Can residents bring their own furniture?
Yes, absolutely. We encourage it. Familiar pieces — a favourite armchair, photographs, a writing desk, art — make a room feel like home. We'll help work out what will fit and arrange the move-in.
03Assisted Living

Life on Broad Oak Lane.

Do I buy or rent the house?
Houses on Broad Oak Lane are available on a 99-year leasehold. We're happy to talk you through the financials, including any options to release equity from your current home.
What does the care cost?
If you choose a care package, the care fee is entirely separate from the cost of the house and is tailored to you — anywhere from one to 24 hours a day. You only pay for what you actually receive, and you can move up or down between Levels One, Two and Three as life changes.
What if my needs change?
This is the central design of the Broad Oak Lane offer. You can start with no care at all and add a package when you're ready — or move between Levels One, Two and Three at any point, up or down, without moving house and without drama.
Can my partner live with me?
Yes. The houses are designed for couples as well as single occupants. Many couples join us with one partner needing more support than the other, and the flexible care levels let each of you have what you need — without having to live separately.
What about pets?
Cats and well-behaved dogs are welcome on Broad Oak Lane. We've had everything from a much-loved spaniel to a tortoise who lives in the back garden. Pets are part of what makes a house a home.
How does this compare to a retirement village?
Broad Oak Lane is smaller, more private, and more carefully cared-for than the typical retirement village. The 29 houses sit within the grounds of a single listed manor, with one team running everything. There are no service charges hidden in a brochure — pricing is plain and on-the-table from the first conversation.
