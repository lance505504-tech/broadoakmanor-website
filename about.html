iv.Our story

We built this home for our own family first — and have run it the same way ever since.

Broad Oak Manor began in 1984, when our family couldn't find the kind of care we wanted for the people we loved. So we made it ourselves. Forty-plus years on, it's still family-owned, still in the same Queen Anne house, and still measured by the only standard that ever mattered to us: care that actually means something to the person receiving it.

01The building

A house with a long memory.

Broad Oak Manor is a Queen Anne, Grade II listed manor house. For most of its life it was a private family home — and you can still feel that in the rooms.

For over forty years we have run it as a nursing home, with great care to preserve what makes the building special while quietly bringing it up to modern standards of care, safety and accessibility.

The gardens are stunning, private, and walkable. Beyond them lie 29 thoughtfully-designed houses on Broad Oak Lane — our assisted-living community.

02Our promise

Three things guide everything we do.

These aren't slogans. They're how we choose the people we hire, the way we train them, the conversations we have with families, and the decisions we make about the home's future.

i.

Independence

Highly trained staff who help you maintain your confidence in your own abilities and your own daily routine.

ii.

iii.

Improvement

We listen carefully to families and refine what we do, year after year.

03Privately owned

Not a chain. A family.

Decisions at Broad Oak Manor are made in the building, by the people who run it. There is no head office, no quarterly target, no acquisition strategy.

This matters more than you might think. It is the reason we can take on a hospital discharge at short notice. The reason a member of staff can sit and read with a resident for an hour.
